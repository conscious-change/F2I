Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about transitioning from federal service to the private sector

How long does the transition typically take?

Most federal employees successfully transition within 3-6 months, though this can vary based on your target industry, level of preparation, and market conditions. Our roadmap helps you create a realistic timeline for your specific situation.

Will I have to take a pay cut when moving to the private sector?

Not necessarily. While compensation structures differ, many federal employees actually see an increase in total compensation when moving to the private sector, especially when factoring in bonuses, equity, and other benefits. Our compensation resources help you understand and negotiate your true market value.

How do I explain my government experience to private sector employers?

Our Skill Translation Hub provides frameworks and tools to help you translate your government experience into terms that resonate with private sector employers, focusing on transferable skills, achievements, and the unique value you bring.

What happens to my federal benefits when I leave?

Our resources include detailed guidance on managing your federal benefits during transition, including options for your pension, TSP, health insurance, and other benefits to ensure you make informed decisions.
